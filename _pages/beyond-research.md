> *"What makes happier and healthier humans."*

> For me, a lot of that happens outdoors.

Outside of economics, I am an endurance-sport enthusiast and a lover of the outdoors: running, cycling, triathlon, skiing, and hiking. Wherever I land, I try to build an active community around me, whether that is a lab running group, an ocean swim, or a weekend hike with my PhD cohort. Friends and I jokingly call it the "outdoor PhD'athlon."

A few highlights:

- 13,500+ km (8,400+ miles) run over 2021 to 2025
- A daily running streak since March 2021, at least 3 km every single day
- Two ultramarathons, three marathons, and a Half-Ironman

## Running

Over the last five years (2021 to 2025) I have run more than 13,500 km (8,400+ miles). Along the way I have completed two 50 km ultramarathons (2023, 2025), three marathons (2024, 2025), and many half-marathons (2019 to 2025), going back to a sub-50-minute 10K in 2018.

I have also kept a personal running streak going since March 2021: at least 3 km (2 miles) every single day.

## Triathlon and cycling

I have raced a Half-Ironman triathlon (Victoria, BC, 2022) and an Olympic-distance triathlon (Vancouver, 2019), trained through the UCSD Triathlon club's ocean swims, and enjoy long days on the bike, including a first 100 km century ride.

## Skiing and hiking

In winter I ski (Whistler Blackcomb, Cypress, Sun Peaks). The rest of the year I hike whenever I can: favorites include Garibaldi Lake and Mt. Elphinstone on the Sunshine Coast, Big Bear (a 2022 PhD cohort trip), and Yosemite.
